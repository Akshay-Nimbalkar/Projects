Early Life:

  • Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam

  • Birth: October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.

  • Family:He came from a modest background, with his father being a boat owner and imam of a local mosque.

  • Education:He studied physics and aerospace engineering, graduating from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1960.

Career in Science:

ISRO & DRDO Contributions:

  • Worked for the Space Research Organization (ISRO) and was a central figure in developing India's civilian space program.

  • Played a pivotal role in India's Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) project, which deployed the Rohini satellite into orbit.

  • Later worked at the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO), where he spearheaded the development of indigenous ballistic missiles, notably the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

  • Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests: In 1998, he was a key figure in India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests, which made India a nuclear-armed state.

Presidency:

  • 11th President of India: Dr. Kalam served as the President from 2002 to 2007. He was widely known as the People’s President because of his humility, vision for India, and his connection with the youth.

  • During his tenure, he inspired millions with his ideas on national development, particularly through his plan Vision 2020, which aimed to transform India into a developed nation by 2020.

Books and Inspiration:

Dr. Kalam was a prolific writer and authored several inspirational books, including:

  • "Wings of Fire"
  • "Ignited Minds"
  • "India 2020"
  • "My Journey"

His works emphasized the importance of education, self-confidence, and scientific advancement.

Contributions to Education:

After his presidency, Dr. Kalam continued to work closely with academic institutions, mentoring students and encouraging them to pursue careers in science and technology.

His vision of empowering the youth through knowledge and innovation remains influential.

Awards and Honors:

  • Bharat Ratna(India’s highest civilian award) in 1997.

  • Padma Bhushan (1981) and Padma Vibhushan (1990).

  • Numerous honorary doctorates and accolades from institutions around the world.

Legacy:

Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong, doing what he loved the most—interacting with students and sharing knowledge.

His life continues to inspire millions across India and the globe, particularly for his humility, dedication to the country, and vision for India’s progress through technology and innovation.
